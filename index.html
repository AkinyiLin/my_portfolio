About Me

Grace Linda is a Geoinformatics graduate from Technical University of Kenya with experience in remote sensing, mapping, spatial data analysis and spatial data visualisation. Projects

Dawa Aquifer Assessment

Dawa aquifer transcending across three East African countries that is Ethiopia, Kenya and Somalia serves as a critical water resource for the three countries by serving local communities.The project included collecting data from primary and secondary sources to map the catchment and aquifer hence providing insights for its assessment.

Completed

Development of TTWCA Digital Circuit Map

Conducted some ground truthing to enhance and validate data for static and digital map designs for TTWCA in Taita Taveta County. Designed well detailed static maps that would later be used in the design of the digital circuit map. The goal was to ensure that there are accurate representations of the attraction sites and the products within the circuits and other points of interest for tourism and conservation efforts.

Completed

Mapping Recurrent Damage on Infrastructure by Cyclones in Australia

Cyclone Alfred hit the Australia's Western side that is Queensland State in March 2025. Properties worth millions were damaged in the process including roads, railway tracks and buildings.This project aimed to quantify the damage due to the cyclone Using data from remote sensing pre and post cylcone mapping was done to highlight regions that were repeatedly affected by cyclone induced flooding.

Completed

Waste Management in Kakamega

From primary data of waste collection sites in Kakamega County, maps were designed to spatially categorize various waste collection sites in the county. The project aimed to ensure that there are accurate locations of various waste collection types to enhance recycling efforts and improve sustainable waste managemnt using GIS.

Completed

School Feeding Project in Kenya and Tanzania

Conducted spatial mapping of project supported schools in both Kenya and Tanzania. Designed well detailed static maps into different thematics categories.

Completed

Somalia Urban Planning

Conducted spatial mapping in some cities in Somalia by extracting building footprints from Open Street Map and segmenting them into various sections using road networks with buffer zones. Calculated the number of buidlings per section to facilitate effiecient fieldwork planning.

Completed

Creation of Rwanda Stormwater Geodatabase

Developed file geodatabase using ArcGIS to store and manage multiple spatial features for stormwater planning project in Rwanda. The geodatabase integrated features that were collected from both primary and secondary sources. The aim of the project was to have a comprehensive geodatabase of all the data of the features that were collected and used with their associated maps for centralized data management.

Completed

Using satelite imagery data such as NDVI, EVI, precipitation, evapotranspiration and wildebeest occurence data in Mara Ecosytem, the link between wildebeest migration and vegetation abundance was assesed. Different maps were generated across various epochs.

Completed

This study used remote sensing data and analytical methods to track historical changes in the mangrove area and measure sand buildup in the bay. Maps were generated to provide preminary products for decision support for the Marereni ecosystem.
